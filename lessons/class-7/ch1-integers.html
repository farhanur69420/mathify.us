Rational Numbers

A rational number is any number of the form $\dfrac{p}{q}$ where $p, q \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $q \neq 0$.

Rational numbers include all integers, fractions, terminating decimals, and recurring decimals.

Ordering Rational Numbers

To compare $\frac{a}{b}$ and $\frac{c}{d}$: convert to common denominator, then compare numerators.

The temperature in Shimla at noon was $8°C$. By midnight it fell by $14°C$. By 6 AM the next morning, it fell another $3°C$. Express each temperature as a rational number and find the range.
1.Noon temperature: $8°C$
2.Midnight: $8 + (-14) = -6°C$
3.6 AM: $-6 + (-3) = -9°C$
4.Range $= 8 - (-9) = 8 + 9 = 17°C$
A trader gains $\frac{3}{4}$ of a rupee on each kg of rice and loses $\frac{1}{6}$ of a rupee on each kg of wheat. He sells 24 kg of rice and 36 kg of wheat. What is his total profit or loss?
1.Gain on rice: $24 \times \frac{3}{4} = 6 \times 3 = \text{₹}18$
2.Loss on wheat: $36 \times \frac{1}{6} = 6 \times 1 = \text{₹}6$
3.Net = $18 - 6 = \text{₹}12$ profit
$\text{Net profit} = 24 \times \dfrac{3}{4} - 36 \times \dfrac{1}{6} = 18 - 6 = \mathbf{₹12}$

Challenge: Comparing Rational Expressions

For each pair, determine which is greater without using decimals.

Problem 1: Compare $\dfrac{-7}{8}$ and $\dfrac{-5}{6}$

1.
LCM of 8 and 6 is 24. So $\dfrac{-7}{8} = \dfrac{-21}{24}$ and $\dfrac{-5}{6} = \dfrac{-20}{24}$
2.
Since $-20 > -21$, we have $\dfrac{-5}{6} > \dfrac{-7}{8}$
3.
Key insight: Among negative fractions, the one with smaller absolute value is greater.

Problem 2: Arrange in ascending order: $\dfrac{3}{-4},\ \dfrac{-5}{8},\ \dfrac{-7}{12},\ \dfrac{1}{-2}$

1.
Convert all to standard form (negative numerator): $\dfrac{-3}{4},\ \dfrac{-5}{8},\ \dfrac{-7}{12},\ \dfrac{-1}{2}$
2.
LCM of 4, 8, 12, 2 is 24. Convert: $\dfrac{-18}{24},\ \dfrac{-15}{24},\ \dfrac{-14}{24},\ \dfrac{-12}{24}$
3.
Ascending: $-18 < -15 < -14 < -12$, so answer is: $\dfrac{3}{-4} < \dfrac{-5}{8} < \dfrac{-7}{12} < \dfrac{1}{-2}$

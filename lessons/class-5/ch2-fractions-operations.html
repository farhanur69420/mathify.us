Word Problems

🚰 Problem 1 (Medium)
A water tank is 3/4 full. During the night, 1/6 of the tank leaked out. What fraction of the tank remains? If the tank holds 120 litres, how many litres remain?
Ask: What do I know?

• Tank starts at 3/4 full
• 1/6 of the total capacity leaked
• Total capacity: 120 litres

Ask: What do I need to find?

• Fraction remaining = 3/4 − 1/6
• Then find 120 × remaining fraction

1
Find LCM of 4 and 6:
Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16...
Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18...
LCM = 12
2
Convert to same denominator:
3/4 = 9/12 (multiply top & bottom by 3)
1/6 = 2/12 (multiply top & bottom by 2)
3
Subtract:
9/12 − 2/12 = 7/12 remains
4
Find litres:
7/12 × 120 = 7 × 10 = 70 litres
🍕 Problem 2 (Medium)
A pizza was cut into 8 equal slices. Priya ate 2/8, Ravi ate 3/8. What fraction did they eat altogether? What fraction is left for their parents?
1
Same denominator — add directly:
2/8 + 3/8 = 5/8 eaten
2
Fraction remaining:
8/8 − 5/8 = 3/8 left
3
Simplify:
3/8 is already in lowest terms (GCD of 3 and 8 is 1)
🏃 Problem 3 (Challenge)
Aryan ran 2/3 of a 15 km track on Saturday and 3/5 of the same track on Sunday. On Monday he ran 1/4 of the total distance from both previous days combined. How far did he run on Monday? (in km)
Multi-step Strategy

Step 1: Find Saturday distance (2/3 × 15)
Step 2: Find Sunday distance (3/5 × 15)
Step 3: Add them
Step 4: Find 1/4 of that sum

1
Saturday: 2/3 × 15 = 10 km
2
Sunday: 3/5 × 15 = 9 km
3
Total Sat + Sun: 10 + 9 = 19 km
4
Monday: 1/4 × 19 = 4.75 km = 4¾ km

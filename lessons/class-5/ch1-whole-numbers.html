🗺 What is BODMAS?

BODMAS tells us the order to perform operations so everyone gets the same answer. Without it, 2 + 3 × 4 could mean 20 or 14 — and only one is correct!

B rackets O rders (powers/roots) D ivision M ultiplication A ddition S ubtraction

Division & Multiplication have equal priority — solve left to right. Same for Addition & Subtraction.

Word Problems

📚 Problem 1 (Easy)
A store has 3 shelves with 12 books each. During the sale, 7 books were sold from the first shelf and 5 from the second shelf. How many books remain in total?
Ask: What do I know?

• 3 shelves × 12 books each
• 7 books sold from shelf 1
• 5 books sold from shelf 2

Ask: What do I need to find?

Total books remaining across all shelves

1
Total books: 3 × 12 = 36 books
Multiplication before subtraction (BODMAS)
2
Books sold: 7 + 5 = 12 books sold
Add all sold books together
3
Remaining: 36 − 12 = 24 books
Subtract sold from total
💡
Expression: 3 × 12 − 7 − 5 = 24
BODMAS: multiply first, then subtract left to right
🛒 Problem 2 (Medium)
A school canteen sold 5 boxes of 24 sandwiches each. Then 3 groups of students (8 students each) each bought 3 sandwiches. How many sandwiches are left?
Build the expression first — then solve

Total − (groups × students × sandwiches) = ?
5 × 24 − 3 × 8 × 3 = ?

1
Expression: 5 × 24 − 3 × 8 × 3
2
Multiply first (BODMAS): 5 × 24 = 120
3
Multiply left to right: 3 × 8 × 3 = 24 × 3 = 72
4
Subtract: 120 − 72 = 48 sandwiches left
🏆 Problem 3 (Challenge)
In a cricket match, Team A scored (45 + 30) × 2 runs in the first half and 4² + 80 ÷ 4 runs in the second half. Team B scored 3 × (52 − 12) + 10 runs total. Which team scored more, and by how much?
A1
Team A first half: (45 + 30) × 2 = 75 × 2 = 150
Brackets first!
A2
Team A second half: 4² + 80 ÷ 4 = 16 + 20 = 36
Orders (4²=16), then Division (80÷4=20), then Addition
A3
Team A total: 150 + 36 = 186 runs
B1
Team B: 3 × (52 − 12) + 10 = 3 × 40 + 10 = 120 + 10 = 130 runs
✅
Team A won by: 186 − 130 = 56 runs

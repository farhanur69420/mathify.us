△ Pythagoras Theorem

One of the most beautiful truths in mathematics — discovered 2,500 years ago and used every day in engineering, navigation, and architecture.

1. The Big Idea — Before Any Formula

Intuition First

Why Does c² = a² + b² Work?

Imagine drawing a right triangle. Now draw a square on each side. The Pythagoras Theorem says something remarkable:

The area of the biggest square equals the combined area of the two smaller squares.

That's it. That's the whole theorem. Click "Animate Proof" below to see it happen.

🌟 Animated Visual Proof — Pythagoras
a² + b² = c²  →  6400 + 3600 = 10000 → c = 100
$$c^2 = a^2 + b^2$$
where c is the hypotenuse (longest side, opposite the right angle)
Key Rule: The hypotenuse is ALWAYS the side opposite the right angle. It is ALWAYS the longest side. You cannot apply Pythagoras without a right angle!

2. Finding the Missing Side

Two Types of Problems

Type 1: Find the Hypotenuse (c)

When you know both shorter sides (a and b), use: $c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$

Type 2: Find a Shorter Side (a or b)

When you know c and one other side, rearrange: $a = \sqrt{c^2 - b^2}$

🔨 Example 1: Ladder Problem — Find the Hypotenuse

▼

Problem: A ladder leans against a wall. Its foot is 5m from the base of the wall, and reaches a height of 12m up the wall. How long is the ladder?

1
Draw and label: Right triangle with legs a = 5m, b = 12m. Find hypotenuse c (the ladder).
2
Write the formula: $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 = 5^2 + 12^2$
3
Calculate: $c^2 = 25 + 144 = 169$
4
Take the square root: $c = \sqrt{169} = 13\text{ m}$
Answer: The ladder is 13 metres long. Notice 5-12-13 is a Pythagorean triple!

🔨 Example 2: Find the Shorter Side

▼

Problem: A ship sails 26km from port, ending up 10km east of the port. How far north did it travel?

1
Identify: Hypotenuse c = 26km (total journey). One leg (east) b = 10km. Find north distance a.
2
Rearrange: $a^2 = c^2 - b^2 = 26^2 - 10^2$
3
Calculate: $a^2 = 676 - 100 = 576$
4
Answer: $a = \sqrt{576} = 24\text{ km}$ north

🔨 Example 3: Singapore Math Multi-Step Challenge

▼

Problem: A rectangular field is 40m long and 30m wide. A farmer wants to fence diagonally across the field and also around the perimeter. Find: (a) the diagonal length, (b) the total fencing needed for both the diagonal and full perimeter, (c) the cost at $8 per metre.

■ Model Drawing
1
Part (a) — Diagonal: $d^2 = 40^2 + 30^2 = 1600 + 900 = 2500$, so $d = 50\text{ m}$
2
Part (b) — Perimeter: $P = 2(40 + 30) = 140\text{ m}$. Total fencing = $140 + 50 = 190\text{ m}$
3
Part (c) — Cost: $190 \times \$8 = \$1520$
Answer: Diagonal = 50m, Total fencing = 190m, Cost = $1,520

3. Pythagorean Triples — Your Calculator Shortcuts

Shortcut

Whole-Number Solutions You Must Memorize

These sets of 3 whole numbers always satisfy $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$. Recognizing them saves enormous time in exams!

3 · 4 · 5
9 + 16 = 25 ✓
5 · 12 · 13
25 + 144 = 169 ✓
8 · 15 · 17
64 + 225 = 289 ✓
7 · 24 · 25
49 + 576 = 625 ✓
6 · 8 · 10
3-4-5 scaled ✓
9 · 40 · 41
81 + 1600 = 1681 ✓
Scaling Rule: Any multiple of a Pythagorean triple is also a triple! So 3-4-5 becomes 6-8-10, 9-12-15, 30-40-50, etc.

4. The Converse — Is This Triangle Right-Angled?

Converse Theorem

Testing Right Angles

If $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$, then the triangle IS right-angled at the angle opposite c.

If $a^2 + b^2 \neq c^2$, then the triangle is NOT right-angled.

5. Singapore Math Challenge Problems

Strategy: For every word problem — (1) Draw a diagram, (2) Label all known values, (3) Identify the right angle, (4) Write the formula, (5) Solve step by step.

🏆 Challenge 1: Two Paths

▼

Problem: From school, Ahmed walks 9km north, then 12km east to reach the library. His friend Sarah takes the straight road directly from school to library. How much shorter is Sarah's route?

1
Visualize: North + East = right angle! Ahmed's path forms the two legs.
2
$d^2 = 9^2 + 12^2 = 81 + 144 = 225$, so $d = 15$ km
3
Ahmed's total = $9 + 12 = 21$ km. Sarah's = 15 km.
Answer: Sarah's route is $21 - 15 = \mathbf{6\text{ km shorter}}$

🏆 Challenge 2: TV Screen Diagonal

▼

Problem: A TV screen has a diagonal of 50 inches. The width is 40 inches. Find (a) the height, (b) the area of the screen, (c) if a frame adds 2 inches to each side, the new outer dimensions.

1
$h^2 = 50^2 - 40^2 = 2500 - 1600 = 900$, so height $= 30$ inches
2
Area $= 40 \times 30 = 1200$ square inches
3
Frame adds 2 on each side: outer width $= 44$ in, outer height $= 34$ in
Answers: Height = 30 in, Area = 1200 in², Outer size = 44 × 34 in

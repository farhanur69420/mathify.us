🔗Number Bonds

Number bonds show us how numbers can be split into two parts. Master the pairs that make 10 and 20 — these are the building blocks of all math!

Core Concept

Part + Part = Whole

A number bond shows how a number (the whole) is made up of two smaller numbers (the parts). It is like a family of three numbers that belong together!

Part + Part = Whole
Example: 3 + 7 = 10  |  6 + 4 = 10  |  8 + 2 = 10

Knowing number bonds helps you add and subtract very quickly — without counting on your fingers!

💡 Key Idea: If you know 3 + 7 = 10, you also know 7 + 3 = 10, 10 − 3 = 7, and 10 − 7 = 3. One bond gives you FOUR facts!

📖 Worked Examples

Example 1 — Finding the missing part

▼

The whole is 10. One part is 6. What is the other part?

1
Write the bond: 6 + ? = 10
2
Count on from 6 until you reach 10: 7, 8, 9, 10. That's 4 more.
3
The missing part is 4. Bond: 6 + 4 = 10.
✅ Answer: The other part is 4.

Example 2 — Using bonds to subtract

▼

10 − 8 = ?

1
Think: 8 + ? = 10 (use your number bond knowledge)
2
You know 8 + 2 = 10, so 10 − 8 = 2.
✅ Answer: 10 − 8 = 2

📝 Word Problems

🍪 Problem 1

Lily has 10 cookies. She eats some and has 3 left. How many did she eat? Draw a number bond to find the answer.

✅ Bond: 3 + ? = 10. She ate 7 cookies.

🌸 Problem 2

In a garden there are 20 flowers. 12 of them are red. The rest are yellow. How many are yellow?

✅ Bond: 12 + ? = 20. Think 12 + 8 = 20. There are 8 yellow flowers.

🎯 Problem 3 (Challenge!)

Jack has some stickers. He gives 9 stickers to his sister and has 11 left. How many stickers did Jack start with?

✅ The whole = 9 + 11 = 20 stickers. Jack started with 20!

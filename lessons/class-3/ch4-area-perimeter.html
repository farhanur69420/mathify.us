Area & Perimeter

Discover the difference between perimeter (the distance around a shape) and area (the space inside). Use formulas and interactive tools to measure rectangles, squares, and composite figures.

What is Perimeter?

The perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a shape. Think of it as the length of a fence you'd need to go around a garden.

Perimeter of Rectangle = 2 × (Length + Width)
Add all four sides: L + W + L + W
Perimeter of Square = 4 × Side
All four sides are equal, so multiply one side by 4

Perimeter is measured in units of length: cm, m, km, etc.

What is Area?

Area is the amount of surface a shape covers. Think of it as how many unit squares (1 cm × 1 cm) fit inside the shape.

Area of Rectangle = Length × Width
Multiply the length by the width to find area in square units (cm², m²)
Area of Square = Side × Side
Since all sides are equal, multiply the side by itself

Area is always measured in square units: cm², m², km².

Word Problem — Bar Model

A rectangular garden has a length of 12 m and a width of 7 m. Priya wants to put a fence all around the garden and also plant grass inside it. How much fencing does she need? What is the area of the garden?

1
Draw the rectangle: Length = 12 m, Width = 7 m
2
Perimeter (fencing) = 2 × (12 + 7) = 2 × 19 = 38 m
3
Area (grass) = 12 × 7 = 84 m²

Priya needs 38 m of fencing and 84 m² of grass.

Composite Shapes

Some shapes are made of two or more rectangles joined together. To find the area, split the shape and add the areas!

Part A (top rectangle): 8 × 3 = 24 cm² Part B (bottom rectangle): 4 × 5 = 20 cm² Total Area = 24 + 20 = 44 cm²

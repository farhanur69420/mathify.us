Mathify is a free, open resource where mathematics and civil engineering meet — built for students, engineers, and self-learners everywhere.

01 —

The Mission

Mathify was created with one simple belief: the gap between understanding a concept and applying it in the real world should be as small as possible. Too often, students encounter engineering mathematics in fragments — a formula here, a derivation there — with no clear bridge between the equation on the board and the structure it describes.

This site is that bridge. From high school calculus to reinforced concrete design, every topic is presented with context, clarity, and intuition — not just symbols and steps.

Whether you're preparing for an exam, working through a design problem, or just curious about how the built world is calculated — this is the place to learn, revisit, and go deeper.

All content is free, permanently. No accounts. No paywalls. No ads. Just learning.

04 —

The Builder

Mathify is built and maintained by Farhan — a civil engineering student at NSU (North South University), developing the site as part of a broader initiative called FarMath: building tools that mirror real engineering workflows and make advanced concepts genuinely accessible.

The goal is to build interactive simulators, calculation tools, and deep-dive guides across every major civil engineering subject — from structural analysis to hydraulics to geotechnical design.

If you have suggestions, corrections, or want to contribute content or translations, the project is open for collaboration.

"The best engineering education doesn't just teach you how to calculate — it teaches you how to think. Mathify exists to build that intuition, one concept at a time."
— Farhan, Builder of Mathify
